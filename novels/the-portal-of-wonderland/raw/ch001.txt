Chapter 1: The Boy from the Fishing Village

Great Qi, Kaiyuan Prefecture, Quanzhou, a remote Fishing Village.

Outside a dilapidated wooden house, a luxuriously decorated carriage was parked, accompanied by several black-clad knights standing with swords. Nearby, many male and female villagers gathered, all with excited expressions, whispering in twos and threes.

"Is it really the Jin family? The Jin family from Feng City?"

"No mistake. Look at the golden emblem embroidered on these people's clothes. Besides the Jin family from Feng City, there couldn't be a second family in the entire Kaiyuan Prefecture."

"Tsk tsk, I didn't expect it! Shi Ting left his wife and child and disappeared for ten years. We thought he had died in another place, but it turns out he climbed up the Jin family and even sent people back to pick up Shi Mu. It's a pity Shi Mu's mother passed away years ago; otherwise, this mother and son would have endless glory and wealth."

"Glory and wealth! That's not necessarily true. Didn't you hear what the Jin family messenger said just now? He said Shi Ting was already the Jin family's son-in-law. If Shi Mu's mother hadn't passed away, Shi Ting wouldn't have dared to send people to pick up Shi Mu."

"No matter what, Shi Mu has finally made it. Yesterday he was an orphan, today he's a young master."

…

"My father is gravely ill in bed, and I have a younger sister?" Shi Mu stared at the blue-shirted old man in front of him and asked slowly.

Only fourteen years old, his skin was slightly dark red from years of exposure to the sea breeze, but he had thick eyebrows and large eyes, and was half a head taller than most peers. Beneath his worn shirt, subtly bulging, well-defined muscles gave him a wild, untamed air.

"Yes, the master is very ill this time and may not make it. That's why the madam sent this old servant to pick up Young Master Shi, so you can see the master one last time," the blue-shirted old man replied with a smile.

"Madam? Since he abandoned my mother for so many years, why bother to acknowledge me as his son now? Go back. I won't go to the Jin family with you," the young man said with a dark expression, without hesitation.

"Ahem! Young Master Shi, I'm afraid you've misunderstood the master. In fact, the master has not returned to the village all these years because he had his reasons. As long as you meet the master in person, all misunderstandings will be cleared up," the old man coughed lightly and added.

"Hmph, no matter how eloquently you speak, it won't change the fact that he abandoned his wife. There's no need to say more," the young man snorted coldly.

The blue-shirted old man finally frowned upon hearing this. After carefully observing the young man for a second time, he seemed to make up his mind and spoke again:

"Young Master, the medicinal herbs you've bought in town these past few years, were they unexpectedly cheap? Also, when Instructor Li from the county martial arts academy taught you martial arts, was his fee much lower than for other Disciples? And the fish and shrimp Young Master Shi caught at sea, were they snatched up as soon as you reached town, with very few people bothering to haggle?"

"You mean, all these things were arranged by him?" the young man's expression changed, and he couldn't help but ask.

"Whether it was personally arranged by the master, I don't know, but these were indeed explicitly ordered by the madam," the blue-shirted old man replied with a smile.

Upon hearing this, the young man's face fluctuated between cloudy and clear, and he hesitated.

"Oh, and Young Master Shi, haven't you always been Cultivating Body Tempering techniques? It seems you must really want to become a true Martial Artist. Before this old servant came, the madam also instructed that as long as Young Master agrees to see the master, she will find a way to get Young Master Shi an Opportunity to enter the Kaiyuan Martial Academy. But whether you can truly be admitted at that time will depend on Young Master Shi's own ability," the blue-shirted old man finally revealed the greatest leverage for his trip.

"Kaiyuan Martial Academy?" The young man was somewhat moved.

"Since the Young Master is a Martial Artist, you must be well aware that the four major martial academies of our country only recruit externally once every five years. The two major conditions for participating in the entrance test are: completing Body Tempering Major Achievement before the age of fifteen, and simultaneously comprehending Qi perception. After all, only those who comprehend Qi perception can Cultivate True Qi, clear their Meridians, and thus become true Martial Artist. However, to comprehend Qi perception, one must take a Qi Spirit Pill to guide it. Its price is so high that even the Jin family can only gather about ten pills each time. If Young Master Shi relies only on himself, he probably won't be able to achieve his wish in this lifetime," the blue-shirted old man continued unhurriedly.

The young man fell silent, and after a long while, he coldly said:

"Come back in three days. I'll give you my answer then."

"Alright, then this old servant will await your good news in three days," Seeing this, the blue-shirted old man knew the young man's mind was made up and did not continue to persuade him. He bowed slightly and took his leave.

…

"Steward Cheng, are we really just going back like this? Why not just use force and take him? Why waste more time?" Not long after the group, with the carriage, left the small Fishing Village, a burly knight couldn't help but ask.

"What nonsense are you talking about? Although Young Master Shi is not a direct descendant of the Jin family, he is the master's flesh and blood. Our purpose this time is to bring him back safely and securely; we must not show any disrespect or neglect. Otherwise, given the affection between the madam and the master, we would absolutely not be able to explain ourselves if they found out," the blue-shirted old man's face immediately darkened as he reprimanded him.

"Yes, this subordinate was muddled," the burly knight became somewhat fearful.

Just then, a figure suddenly flashed by the roadside. Another black-clad knight, dressed similarly, darted out. After bowing to the old man, he hurriedly said:

"Steward Cheng, the brothers found Fifth Master's Biao brothers nearby. How do you wish to deal with them?"

"Hmph, it seems Fifth Master hasn't given up on his original plan. The Biao brothers are both at the Acquired Early Stage; only I can deal with them. You few wait here. You lead the way, I'll go personally and resolve them," the blue-shirted old man frowned, then decisively said.

"Yes."

The knight moved, immediately turning to run back the way they came.

The blue-shirted old man, with a "whoosh," floated directly off his horse and rushed away with the knight. His figure was incredibly light, as if he had no weight at all, and in a short while, he disappeared into the nearby woods.

The burly knight and the remaining few continued to cluster around the carriage, staying obediently in place.

…

In the evening, on an unnamed mound about a mile outside the Fishing Village, under the faint moonlight, Shi Mu knelt alone before a slightly raised grave. On a light yellow stone tablet in front of the grave, the large black characters "Tomb of the Wang Clan of Shimen" were clearly inscribed.

"Mother, you never expected this, did you? Father isn't dead; instead, he married into another family outside. But don't worry, I will definitely fulfill the promise I made. Father left you under the pretext of pursuing his Martial Artist dream. Then, Mu'er will also become a true Martial Artist, and the strongest Martial Artist in this world, so that you can rest in peace in the netherworld!"

The young man murmured a few words in front of the grave, then stood up. After stretching his limbs, he began to practice boxing in front of the grave.

With the "crack-crack" sound of bones, the young man's movements gradually accelerated from slow to fast. His body was like a tiger's. After about a quarter of an hour, only swirling fists could be seen, and the surrounding dirt was stirred up into a yellow dust cloud, completely engulfing the young man's figure.

"Whoosh!"

A figure burst out from the morning mist, striking a nearby tree, about the size of a bowl, with a powerful punch.

With a loud "boom," the tree shook, countless branches and leaves fell, and a half-inch deep fist print was clearly visible on the trunk.

Shi Mu stared at the fist print on the tree trunk, his eyebrows raised.

This set of boxing techniques was the only one he learned from the town's martial arts academy, and it was also the most widely circulated Body Tempering martial art in Great Qi.

According to the martial arts instructor, the fact that he could leave such a deep mark on the tree trunk meant he should now be at Body Tempering Seventh Layer, only two layers away from true Body Tempering Major Achievement, which would allow him to undergo the rumored Qi perception comprehension.

And this had taken him a full four years!

One must know, the saying "poor scholars, rich warriors" was absolutely not without reason.

Since he decided to start Cultivating martial arts, just the thirty taels of silver for the martial arts academy's entrance fee took him a full five or six months to save up, and that fee was already much lower than for other Disciples because of his repeated pleas.

And the cost of numerous medicinal baths after starting Body Tempering consumed all his fishing income for these past few years, often leaving him penniless and his home completely destitute.

Even so, Shi Mu's ability to Cultivate to such a high level of Body Tempering had already greatly astonished everyone at the martial arts academy. Several instructors repeatedly marveled, believing his bone structure was excellent, and he was a natural talent for martial arts.

After all, during this period, although he intermittently asked the martial arts academy's pharmacists to prepare medicinal decoctions to cleanse his body, with that amount of money in town, how could he really buy any Spiritual Plant? Most were just ordinary medicinal herbs, and their Body Tempering effect was negligible.

Shi Mu sighed in his heart as he thought of this.

He knew very well that his bone structure was very ordinary. The reason he could reach his current Body Tempering level in just a few years was due to another Opportunity two years ago.

This was also why he didn't immediately agree to go to the Jin family with the blue-shirted old man earlier.

Thinking of this, the young man looked up at the moon in the sky, then turned and ran down the mountain.

A quarter of an hour later, Shi Mu appeared by the sea, where jagged reefs abounded. With a "plop," he directly jumped into the seawater. His arms moved, his body twisted, and he silently dove down rapidly, like a large fish.

In a short while, he reached a depth of thirty to forty zhang in the seawater.

An astonishing scene appeared.

Below the originally dark seawater, faint specks of white light began to appear, growing more numerous and brighter, illuminating the surrounding water.

Shi Mu was not surprised by this. He held his breath, quickly moved his limbs a few more meters downward, then suddenly flipped his body upside down. His feet abruptly landed on the sandy bottom, reaching an area of fine white sand on the seabed.

Only then could one see clearly that the objects emitting faint white light around the seabed were more than a dozen palm-sized shells, surrounding a huge reef seven or eight zhang high, flickering on and off.
